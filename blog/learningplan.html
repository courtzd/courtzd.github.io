Bootcamp Learning Plan

What is your long term goal or career pathway?

My long term goal is to continue learning and growing in coding arena. Again i still don't have a definitive career plan, as I am doing this programme more as a self interest personal development. Proving to myself that I can learn new things and the challenges that come with it. So even though i don't have a specific destination in mind, it doesn't mean something along the way won't pop up and i follow that pathway.

What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?

Foundations allowed me to work through my perfectionism and just get things done, it was hard but i definitely know i let go in some parts to ensure i met time targets.

I dont think the limitations I noted down in foundations will change, they still limit me, i have just gotten a little bit better at managing them.

Breaking my foot and injuring myself at the beginning of bootcamp, has definitely set me back. Mentally and physically. But i am proud of the fact that i said something to my facilators. I'm still here to succed even if the plan has to be adjust slightly, which it has as i have moved to part time.

What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

Staying focussed will definitely be a big one. I had full time boot camp all kind of panned out in my head and had plans in place for support. So changing to part time i'm going to have to re-evaluate my plan and continue to push.

What non-technical skills - human skills - would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

Definitely working in teams, pair-programming with others. I'd like to see me grow in this area, because it would help me deal with not being always in control. I'm using to managing other people more than working alongside them. So i am looking forward to seeing how i grow in this area the most, it both excites me and terrifies me.

What are your expectations from the Bootcamp team?

Same as foundations, that the support is there when needed. That they are approachable, and open.

What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp?

As cliche as it sounds, but i expect myself to always strive to do my best at all times. Be committed, be present and be involved.

  • Being present - is attending any required classes or meetings Being involved - is having the willingness to contribute to discussions, group work and getting my work done. Being committed - Is about being honest with myself and others, if im struggling then I need to say something sooner rather than later.

Difficulty in seeking help: I do find this challenging as I am a self motivated person who will seek answers in my own way.
